The Factory Weak Point

Plastic Expands.
Rubber Splits.
Aluminum Does Not.

Factory Plastic Cracks Under Boost
The factory 2011-2016 cold-side pipe is plastic. Heat cycles, boost pressure, and vibration cause it to weaken, expand, and eventually crack — killing boost and killing power.
2017+ Rubber Sections Split
The 2017-2026 factory pipe uses a combination of plastic and rubber sections. The rubber splits and the plastic connector near the throttle body cracks — same result: massive boost leak.
Silicone Competitors Expand Too
Many aftermarket kits replace aluminum with oversized silicone. Silicone can billow and expand under boost, disrupting airflow consistency. More silicone ≠ more performance.

→Install Guide

Install in Under an Hour.
No Shop Required.

The Cold Side Charge Pipe is engineered for straightforward installation. Basic hand tools, your driveway, and 45–60 minutes. No cutting, no modifications, no fabrication.

01
1Step One

Let the Engine Cool Down

Allow the engine to cool completely. Remove any heat shields or covers blocking access to the cold-side charge pipe — this is the pipe running from the intercooler outlet to the intake manifold / throttle body.

02
2Step Two

Remove the Factory Pipe

Remove the factory clamps and slide off the OEM plastic or rubber cold-side pipe. Clean the intercooler outlet and intake manifold mating surfaces. Install the included new O-ring and retaining clip on the throttle body port.

03
3Step Three

Install SD Pipe & Seal It Up

Slide the Sinister Diesel aluminum pipe into position. Install the 5-ply silicone boot with No-Slip Lip and torque the T-bolt clamps. Start the truck, confirm no boost leaks, and enjoy the upgrade. Done.
