The Factory Problem

Your OEM Housing
Is a Fire Hazard.

The factory 6.7L plastic fuel filter housing is one of the most common leak points on these trucks — cracking, warping, and leaking directly into the engine bay.

Plastic Housing Cracks
Repeated heat cycles cause the factory plastic to warp and crack — often without warning. It's not a matter of if, it's when.
Diesel Fuel Leaks
A cracked or warped housing leaks diesel fuel directly into the engine bay. This is a serious fire risk and requires immediate attention.
Competitor Kits Add Risk
Many aftermarket kits use stacked adapters and multiple connection points — creating more potential leak points than the OEM they're replacing.

Install in 3 Steps.
No Shop. No Tuner.

The Fuel Filter Conversion Kit is designed for the home mechanic. Basic tools, your driveway, and about 30–45 minutes is all it takes to permanently fix Ford's known weak point.

01
1Step One

Remove the OEM Housing

Drain the fuel system and unbolt the factory plastic filter housing. Disconnect the OEM quick-connect fuel lines. The housing pulls straight off — no special tools needed.

02
2Step Two

Install the SD Housing

Drop the Sinister Diesel billet housing onto the factory mounting location. Reconnect the factory fuel lines to the redesigned quick-connect fittings. No adapters. No cutting. Direct fit.

03
3Step Three

Install Filter & You're Done.

Install the included Baldwin BF7967 filter, prime the fuel system, and start your truck. The factory fuel filter housing problem is now permanently solved. No revisiting required.
